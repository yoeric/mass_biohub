The WPI-MBI BioHub is closing three gaps that have held back bioindustrial manufacturing in Central Massachusetts: shared **pilot-scale capacity**, data-driven **process optimization**, and **workforce training** designed for non-therapeutic biomanufacturing. We're building that infrastructure now — some is available today, more is coming online over the next three years.

## Network {#network}

The BioHub is more than a facility — it's a network of regional and national partners working together to grow bioindustrial manufacturing in Central Massachusetts. We help members find the right collaborators, equipment, faculty expertise, and funding pathways for their projects. Formal touchpoints include twice-yearly BioHub Network meetings, quarterly Leadership Council sessions, an Advisory Board, and an annual BioHub Day.

If you're not sure what you need, start with us — we'll help you map it out.

## Projects {#projects}

The BioHub is designed to support three families of project work. Each draws on dedicated infrastructure and faculty expertise. Some capabilities are available today through WPI CERES; the full project portfolio comes online as pilot infrastructure is built out.

<div class="cards">
  <div class="card">
    <h3 class="h3">Process Development</h3>
    <p>Bioprocess development and optimization from bench to pilot readiness, including media and feeding strategy, fermentation design, and downstream unit operations.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="card">
    <h3 class="h3">Scale-Up</h3>
    <p>Scale-down models, tech transfer readiness, and reproducible data packages for go/no-go decisions and investor-grade demonstrations.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="card">
    <h3 class="h3">Strain Engineering</h3>
    <p>Automated design-build-test-learn (DBTL) workflows for rapid strain construction, screening, and AI-driven optimization.</p>
  </div>
</div>

### Available today

The BioHub's current project capabilities are anchored at **WPI CERES** (Center for Excellence in Biomedical Research and Science), WPI's established research facility at Gateway Park in Worcester. CERES provides access to bioreactor equipment and faculty expertise for early-stage process work — the foundation on which the BioHub's full project portfolio is being built.

<details>
  <summary>Current CERES equipment &amp; capabilities</summary>
  <ul>
    <li>Beckman Coulter BioLector microbioreactor (received Q1 2026, commissioning underway)</li>
    <li>WPI faculty expertise in bioprocess development, strain engineering, and fermentation science</li>
    <li>Laboratory-scale fermentation and analytical equipment</li>
  </ul>
</details>

### Coming online over the next three years

As physical space is secured and equipment is installed, the following resources will expand the BioHub's project capacity substantially:

<details>
  <summary>Planned equipment &amp; specs — BioFoundry</summary>
  <p>An expansion of the CERES facility for high-throughput strain engineering. Will feature automated DBTL workflows with data sufficient to train AI models for strain and process optimization.</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Two Sartorius Ambr250 systems (planned, target Q2 2026)</li>
    <li>Liquid-handling automation for parallel DBTL cycles (planned)</li>
  </ul>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Planned equipment &amp; specs — Scale-Up Lab (Pilot Facility)</summary>
  <p>A 10,000 sq ft pilot facility designed to support both aerobic and anaerobic fermentation with integrated downstream operations. Construction planned to begin Q2 2026; first scale-up projects targeted Q3 2026.</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Bioreactors from 2L through 150L (planned)</li>
    <li>Downstream operations: harvest, centrifugation, membrane filtration, evaporation, spray drying (planned)</li>
    <li>Automated sampling, real-time sensor networks, and deep-learning process models (planned)</li>
  </ul>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Planned equipment &amp; specs — Builder's Lab</summary>
  <p>A 4,500 sq ft prototyping space operated with MBI where process-technology startups will be able to design, build, and test new bioprocessing equipment alongside potential end-users. Target build-out Q3 2026.</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Shared rapid-prototyping equipment (planned)</li>
    <li>Co-location with MBI to connect equipment developers with the regional bioindustrial network (planned)</li>
  </ul>
</details>

## Workforce Development {#workforce}

Bioindustrial manufacturing requires skills that aren't well covered by existing biopharmaceutical training programs. The BioHub is developing three competency-based tracks to fill that gap, in partnership with regional educators including Worcester-area community colleges and universities:

<div class="cards">
  <div class="card">
    <h3 class="h3">Bioreactor Operation</h3>
    <p>Hands-on training across all scales, from microbioreactors to pilot-scale fermenters.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="card">
    <h3 class="h3">Remote Monitoring</h3>
    <p>Digital-twin and AR/VR-enabled instruction in remote process operation and monitoring.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="card">
    <h3 class="h3">Downstream Processing</h3>
    <p>Practical skills in harvesting, separation, and purification of fermentation products.</p>
  </div>
</div>

These tracks will support job seekers, mid-career retraining candidates, and incumbent workers shifting from related industries. A Bioindustrial Process Operation Certificate is planned for launch in Year 3 of the initiative.

If you're an educator, employer, or trainee interested in our programs, [get in touch]({{ '/contact/' | relative_url }}).
