The BioHub closes three gaps that have held back bioindustrial manufacturing in Central Massachusetts: shared **pilot-scale capacity**, data-driven **process optimization**, and **workforce training** designed for non-therapeutic biomanufacturing. Some capabilities are available today through WPI CERES; the full portfolio comes online as pilot infrastructure is built out over the next three years.

## Scale your process {#scale}

The **BioHub Pilot Facility** is being designed to support both aerobic and anaerobic fermentation with integrated downstream operations — closing the gap between bench-scale results and a manufacturable process. AI-integrated sensors and automated sampling enable real-time process modeling and reproducible scale-up data.

The **MBI Builder's Lab** complements the Pilot Facility with shared prototyping space for process-technology startups designing new bioreactor and unit-operation hardware — co-located with potential users for fast feedback cycles.

<details>
  <summary>Planned equipment &amp; specs — Pilot Facility</summary>
  <p>A 10,000 sq ft pilot facility supporting both aerobic and anaerobic fermentation. Construction planned to begin Q2 2026; first scale-up projects targeted Q3 2026.</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Bioreactors from 1L through 150L (planned)</li>
    <li>Downstream operations: harvest, centrifugation, membrane filtration, evaporation, spray drying (planned)</li>
    <li>Automated sampling, real-time sensor networks, and AI-driven process control (planned)</li>
    <li>Anaerobic-rated workstations and bioreactors for emerging microbiome applications</li>
  </ul>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Planned equipment &amp; specs — Builder's Lab</summary>
  <p>A 4,500 sq ft prototyping space operated with MBI where process-technology startups can design, build, and test new bioprocessing equipment alongside potential end users. Target build-out Q3 2026.</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Shared rapid-prototyping equipment (planned)</li>
    <li>Co-location with MBI to connect equipment developers with the regional bioindustrial network</li>
  </ul>
</details>

## Engineer your strain {#engineer}

The **WPI BioFoundry** is an expansion of WPI's CERES facility for high-throughput strain engineering. Automated DBTL (design-build-test-learn) cycles generate the data needed to train AI models for strain and process optimization — letting teams iterate faster on host selection, pathway design, and production conditions.

### Available today
The BioFoundry builds on **WPI CERES** (Cell Engineering Research Equipment Suite), an established research facility at Gateway Park serving WPI faculty and partner companies. Early-stage process work — including bioprocess development, strain engineering, and laboratory-scale fermentation — is available through CERES today.

<details>
  <summary>Current CERES equipment &amp; capabilities</summary>
  <ul>
    <li>Beckman Coulter BioLector microbioreactor (commissioning underway)</li>
    <li>Multi-user automated liquid handling for parallel DBTL workflows</li>
    <li>WPI faculty expertise in bioprocess development, strain engineering, and fermentation science</li>
    <li>Laboratory-scale fermentation and analytical equipment</li>
  </ul>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Planned equipment &amp; specs — BioFoundry expansion</summary>
  <ul>
    <li>Two Sartorius Ambr250 systems (planned, target Q2 2026)</li>
    <li>Expanded liquid-handling automation for parallel DBTL cycles (planned)</li>
    <li>Microbioreactor scale-down studies linked to pilot-scale data for de-risked scale-up</li>
  </ul>

## Train your team {#train}

Bioindustrial manufacturing requires skills that aren't well covered by existing biopharmaceutical training programs. The BioHub is developing three competency-based tracks to fill that gap — in partnership with regional educators including Worcester-area community colleges and universities — and will scale instruction through digital-twin and AR/VR-enabled curriculum.

<div class="cards">
  <div class="card">
    <h3 class="h3">Bioreactor Operation</h3>
    <p>Hands-on training across all scales, from microbioreactors to pilot-scale fermenters.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="card">
    <h3 class="h3">Remote Monitoring</h3>
    <p>Digital-twin and AR/VR-enabled instruction in remote process operation and monitoring.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="card">
    <h3 class="h3">Downstream Processing</h3>
    <p>Practical skills in harvesting, separation, and purification of fermentation products.</p>
  </div>
</div>

These tracks support job seekers, mid-career retraining candidates, and incumbent workers shifting from related industries. A **Bioindustrial Process Operation Certificate** is planned for launch in Year 3 of the initiative.
